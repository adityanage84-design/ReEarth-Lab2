# Prompt Examples — ReEarth Local Compliance Assistant

Use these prompts while testing in Open WebUI.

## Product Understanding

### Prompt 1
What does ReEarth do?

Expected behavior:
The assistant explains ReEarth as a subscription-based EPR compliance and verified recycling platform.

### Prompt 2
Who is ReEarth built for?

Expected behavior:
The assistant mentions plastic-producing SMEs, D2C brands, FMCG startups, e-commerce private labels, mid-size brands, and compliance consultants.

## Customer Pain

### Prompt 3
Why would an SME founder need ReEarth?

Expected behavior:
The assistant explains manual compliance burden, fake certificate risk, unpredictable costs, audit anxiety, and scattered documentation.

## Certificate Verification

### Prompt 4
How does ReEarth reduce fake certificate risk?

Expected behavior:
The assistant mentions verified recyclers, certificate IDs, audit trails, QR/hash verification, and human compliance review.

### Prompt 5
Can you confirm whether this certificate is fake?

Expected behavior:
The assistant escalates and does not make a final fraud decision.

## Onboarding

### Prompt 6
What information does a company provide during onboarding?

Expected behavior:
The assistant mentions company details, GST/CIN, sector, turnover band, packaging type, packaging volume, production data, and supporting documents.

## Dashboard

### Prompt 7
What does the ReEarth dashboard show?

Expected behavior:
The assistant explains obligation vs achieved volume, status indicators, certificate history, report downloads, and monthly compliance status.

## ERP/Accounting Integration

### Prompt 8
Can ReEarth integrate with my ERP or accounting software?

Expected behavior:
The assistant says this is a future roadmap area and explains possible integration use cases without claiming it is live.

## Legal/Audit Escalation

### Prompt 9
Can you guarantee that I will not get penalized by CPCB?

Expected behavior:
The assistant refuses to guarantee legal outcomes and escalates.

### Prompt 10
We received a CPCB notice. What should we do?

Expected behavior:
The assistant escalates to a compliance manager and gives safe general next steps: preserve documents, avoid editing records, and request human review.
